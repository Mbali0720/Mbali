About GreenFuture SA

Our team and volunteers after a tree planting event in Chesterville township

Our Story

GreenFuture SA was founded in 2021 by three environmental science students at the University of KwaZulu-Natal. What started as a small tree-planting initiative has grown into a registered NPO serving 8 townships across Durban and Pietermaritzburg.

It all began with a single tree-planting event in Chesterville township, where 50 young people showed up on a Saturday morning to plant 200 indigenous trees. The energy and commitment of those volunteers showed us that township youth are ready to take climate action - they just need opportunities and support.

Today, we operate in 8 townships including Chesterville, KwaMashu, Umlazi, Clermont, Imbali, Mpumuza, Sobantu, and Eastwood. We have planted over 5,000 trees, trained more than 300 youth in environmental leadership and green skills, and established 15 school eco-clubs.

In 2024, we expanded our programmes to include recycling workshops and youth climate camps. Our holistic approach ensures that young people not only learn about environmental issues but also gain practical skills that can lead to green job opportunities.

Our Mission

To empower youth in South African townships to take climate action through environmental education, tree planting, and recycling initiatives. We believe that young people have the power to create lasting change in their communities.

Our Vision

A South Africa where every township community is green, clean, and climate-resilient. We envision a future where young people lead the way in protecting our environment and building sustainable communities.

Our Core Values

Youth-led

Young people are at the centre of everything we do. They plan, lead, and implement our projects.

Community-first

We work with, not for, township communities. Local leaders help us identify priorities.

Sustainability

Our projects are designed to last. We train local volunteers who continue the work.

Transparency

We are accountable to our donors, volunteers, and the communities we serve. Our finances are published annually.

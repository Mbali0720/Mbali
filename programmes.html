Our Programmes

Three ways we take climate action in townships. All programmes are completely free for participants.

Flagship Programme

Tree Planting Initiative

Weekly events | 3 hours per session | All equipment provided

We plant indigenous trees in schools, churches, and public spaces. Each tree planting event includes a workshop on why trees matter for fighting climate change, preventing soil erosion, and providing shade in township communities that often lack green spaces.

What participants learn:

  • How to plant and care for indigenous trees
  • The role of trees in fighting climate change
  • How to start a community tree nursery
  • Environmental leadership skills

Impact so far: 5,000+ trees planted in 8 townships. Each tree absorbs approximately 22kg of CO2 per year.

Skills Development

Recycling and Upcycling Workshops

6-week course | 2 hours per week | Includes starter kit

We teach young people how to turn waste (plastic bottles, old tyres, cardboard) into reusable products like plant pots, furniture, and school supplies. Some participants have started small recycling businesses selling upcycled products at local markets.

What participants learn:

  • How to sort and process different types of waste
  • Techniques for turning waste into sellable products
  • Basic business and marketing skills
  • How to start a recycling micro-enterprise

Impact so far: 200 youth trained, 5 micro-enterprises started, over 10 tons of waste diverted from landfills.

Leadership Development

Youth Climate Camps

Weekend camps | 2 nights | Includes meals and materials

Weekend camps where young people learn about climate science, environmental advocacy, and green job skills. Camp graduates become Eco-Champions in their schools and communities, leading their own environmental projects with our support.

What participants learn:

  • Climate science and environmental advocacy
  • Project planning and leadership skills
  • How to start and run an eco-club
  • Green job pathways and career opportunities

Impact so far: 3 camps held, 120 youth graduated as Eco-Champions, 8 new school eco-clubs established by graduates.
